Data Overview

The dataset contains 2000 rows, each representing a customer, with 8 features:

Feature Description
Customer IDUnique identifier for each customer.
GenderGender of the customer.
AgeAge of the customer.
Annual IncomeAnnual income of the customer.
Spending ScoreA score based on the customer's spending behavior.
ProfessionCustomer's profession.
Work ExperienceYears of experience in their profession.
Family SizeSize of the customer's family.

What is Clustering?

Clustering is an unsupervised machine learning technique that groups data points based on similarity. It helps uncover natural groupings in data without predefined labels.

In this project, clustering is used to identify customer segments with similar shopping behaviors, which can help businesses target specific groups more effectively.

I used K-Means Clustering, a popular method that divides data into k groups by minimizing the distance between points and their respective group centers.

Data Preprocessing

Before clustering, the data was cleaned and prepared:

  • Handled missing values by filling or dropping them.
  • Encoded categorical variables (Gender and Profession) using One-Hot Encoding.
  • Standardized numerical features like Age and Income to ensure all features were on the same scale.

Data Exploration and Visualization

I analyzed the distribution of key variables such as Age, Annual Income, and Spending Score. Visualizations like histograms, scatter plots, and boxplots helped identify patterns and potential outliers.

Modeling: K-Means Clustering

I used the Elbow Method and Silhouette Analysis to determine the optimal number of clusters. Based on the results, I chose 6 clusters and fit the K-Means model to the data.

Clustering Analysis

Each cluster can be interpreted as follows:

  • Cluster 1: Older individuals with decent income who spend moderately.
  • Cluster 2: Middle-aged high earners who spend heavily.
  • Cluster 3: Middle-aged low-income individuals with minimal spending.
  • Cluster 4: Wealthy but conservative spenders.
  • Cluster 5: Older moderate earners with low spending.
  • Cluster 6: Younger low-income group with minimal spending.

Impact and Ethical Considerations

This project can help businesses fine-tune their marketing and product strategies for different customer groups. However, collecting and using personal demographic data raises privacy concerns. Also, missing factors like interests, values, and lifestyles mean clustering alone won't fully explain customer behavior.
