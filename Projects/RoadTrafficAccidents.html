Project Two: Road Traffic Accident Classification

The Problem

Words

Using the Road Traffic Accidents Dataset , this project aims to answer:

  • How does smoking affect key health risk factors such as cholesterol levels and heart attack risk?
  • What is the relationship between sedentary hours and heart attack risk?

This project aims to explore meaningful patterns in heart attack risk and contribute to a better understanding of how lifestyle factors play a role.

Dataset Information

The dataset contains 12316 rows, each representing instances of an accident, and 32 features. These features are important variables in determining the likelihood of a road traffic accident. The features include:

Feature Explanation
Age_band_of_driver The age group of the driver involved in the accident.
Sex_of_driver The gender of the driver (male/female).
Educational_level The education level of the driver.
Vehicle_driver_relation The relationship between the driver and the vehicle (e.g., owner, employee).
Driving_experience The number of years the driver has been driving.
Lanes_or_Medians The type of lanes or medians present on the road where the accident occurred.
Types_of_Junction The type of road junction where the accident took place.
Road_surface_type The type of road surface at the accident location (e.g., asphalt, gravel).
Light_conditions The lighting conditions at the time of the accident (e.g., daylight, night, streetlights).
Weather_conditions The weather conditions at the time of the accident (e.g., clear, rain, fog).
Type_of_collision The nature of the collision (e.g., rear-end, head-on, sideswipe).
Vehicle_movement The movement of the vehicle at the time of the accident (e.g., parked, overtaking, turning).
Pedestrian_movement The movement of pedestrians involved in the accident (if applicable).
Cause_of_accident The reported cause of the accident (e.g., speeding, distraction, mechanical failure).
Accident_severity The severity of the accident (e.g., minor, serious, fatal).
